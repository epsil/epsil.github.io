Do You Want to Become an IEEE Author?

Vegard Øye • 2007-08-08

Suppose you want to publish something that is as simple as

\[\label{eq:orig} 1 + 1 = 2\]

This is not very impressive. If you want your article to be accepted by reviewers, you have to be more abstract. So, you could complicate the left hand side of the expression by using

\[1= \ln(\mathrm{e}) \quad\text{and}\quad 1 = \sin^2 x + \cos^2 x\]

The right hand side can be stated as

\[2 = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{1}{2^n}\]

Therefore, eq. (\(\ref{eq:orig}\)) can be expressed more “scientifically” as:

\[\label{eq:mid} \ln(\mathrm{e}) + (\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x) = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{1}{2^n}\]

which is far more impressive. However, you should not stop here. The expression can be further complicated by using

\[1 = \cosh(y) \sqrt{1 – \tanh^2(y)} \quad\text{and}\quad \mathrm{e} = \lim_{z \to \infty}\left(1 + \frac{1}{z}\right)^z\]

Eq. (\(\ref{eq:mid}\)) may therefore be written as

\[\ln\left[\lim_{z \to \infty}\left(1 + \frac{1}{z}\right)^z\right] + (\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x) = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{\cosh(y)\sqrt{1 – \tanh^2(y)}}{2^n}\]

Note: Other methods of a similar nature could also be used to enhance your prestige, once you grasp the underlying principles.
